My name's Flora and I'm currently a student at RMIT University studying design in digital media. I call Melbourne (Australia) my home and aspire to help people who help make the lives of others a little better.

I mainly work with UI/UX, websites and motion graphics, yet I admire many types of creative work, such as architecture and interior design.

All projects featured on my portfolio are original and received a high distinction (the highest possible letter grade) in an academic setting.

The Moon Is Made Of Cheese is a title sequence for a hypothetical sci-fi comedy sitcom that features 2D animations influenced by the physics of surrealism to create a sense of playfulness and curiosity through lively and bouncy movements.


Completed — June 2020
Type — Title sequence
Format — Video

An anxious soul, who unconsciously alternates between two worlds when she wakes up after sleeping, wishes to only stay in one.

However, when her dreams come true and she is stuck there, she begins to question its reality.

Now, she must find where she belongs.

Using contrasting colours, a hand-drawn style, and visual juxtapositions, The Moon Is Made Of Cheese plays with reflections, shadows and mirrors to express the character's wavering worlds.

The soundtrack, remixed from "The Mountain" by Podington Bear from the Free Music Archive, supports the idea of having two sides of the same coin by containing low and calming plucks that conflict with vibrant and jumpy chords. The gradual layering and increase in pitch also reflects the character's desire of wanting more.

The narrative of the title sequence was constructed around the 4 bars of the soundtrack. Because the soundtrack is divided into 4 sections, each part of the animation represents a piece of the character's journey which is differentiated by the colour palette.

As the title sequence opens, it introduces the character's 2 worlds in navy and white, and depicts it splitting into 2. Next, orange and yellow illuminate the character's freeing abilities using organic lines and soft objects. Contrary to this, the next scenes in blue and green portrays the character's struggles, doubt, and confinement with geometric shapes and heavy objects. At the end, The Moon Is Made Of Cheese concludes with the notion of balance and discovery by turning the moon into cheese.

Wander

My name's Flora and I'm currently a student at RMIT University studying design in digital media. I call Melbourne (Australia) my home and aspire to help people who help make the lives of others a little better.

I mainly work with UI/UX, websites and motion graphics, yet I admire many types of creative work, such as architecture and interior design.

All projects featured on my portfolio are original and received a high distinction (the highest possible letter grade) in an academic setting.

Portfolio designed and built (with assistance) by Flora. Contact me via email.

Working alongside the existing brand of Script Skincare, Wander is a series of ambient, loopable, pre-rendered videos that were proposed to enhance visual communication and reinforce the idea of a journey in Script's concept store.


Completed — October 2019
Type — Digital signage
Format — Video
View all — Here

Script Skincare is an Australian business who simplify the search of skincare products by matching customers with cosmeceuticals that work to help achieve their skin goals.

Inspired by the journey of the customer as they explore the store and go on a quest towards their ambitions, Wander elegantly reinterprets and reflects Script's icons, colours and identity into ambient animations through lines, texture and layers, much like the layers of the skin, the layers of the Earth, and the elements of the Earth.

Since the screens in the store were each placed at different sections, from the welcome area, to the product displays, into consultation rooms and to the checkout, it was clear to design separate animated videos for each screen. Additional text was also embedded into some of the videos to educate customers on Script's philosophy.

Subtly inspired by linework in Art Nouveau pieces, Wander was created using Adobe After Effects and Illustrator.

Welcome

The 'welcome' screen, which would be the first one to be seen by visitors, is light and ethereal, like air, to ease customers into the store. To introduce Script Skincare, text about their services is fixed into the video. Featuring beige tones to not clash with the bold interior blue, soft and organic lines that imply the uncertainty and potential for change tell the story at the beginning of the skincare journey.

'Welcome' screen moodboard

Product

Next, the 'product' screen represents water, mirrors and reflections to resonate with the shiny surfaces of science and technology involved with Script's skin profiling methods. There are calming organic lines that make up the slightly more textural components, gradual shades of blue to complement the interior, as well as text to accompany the impression of being pointed in the right direction.

'Product' screen moodboard

Consultation

Afterwards, the 'consultation' screen that sits in the private rooms has the theme of foliage. It has the most texture, and is filled with pattern and repetition, despite being placed third in the journey as it is an optional step where there is little foot traffic and movement in the room. Because of the presence of experts, there is no text based element so that customers are able to be educated directly from the knowledgeable staff themselves.

'Consultation' screen moodboard

Pay

Finally, the 'pay' screen exhibits a sense of security and stability in pink tones and a balance of organic and geometric lines. To farewell customers as they checkout and leave them with confidence, the idea of a strong foundation, like earth, is echoed with an inspiring message. There is an anticipation for the future with a play on light to ultimately support Script and their customers in their skincare journey.

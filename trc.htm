Wander

My name's Flora and I'm currently a student at RMIT University studying design in digital media. I call Melbourne (Australia) my home and aspire to help people who help make the lives of others a little better.

I mainly work with UI/UX, websites and motion graphics, yet I admire many types of creative work, such as architecture and interior design.

All projects featured on my portfolio are original and received a high distinction (the highest possible letter grade) in an academic setting.

Portfolio designed and built (with assistance) by Flora. Contact me via email.

The Reading Club is an interactive website that explores the notion of the body, particularly in the brain where our reading habits have altered to fit the rapid processes of the Internet due to the overuse of digital technology.


Completed — April 2019
Type — Desktop website
Visit — Here
Optimised for — Google Chrome, 1920 x 1080 screen

(Please adjust your screen accordingly by zooming in or out until the buttons fit into the boxes)

Users are introduced to a hypothetical narrative of an imaginary book club where they must complete a questionnaire in order to successfully join the online community. These questions take the form of 3D rectangular prisms, which mimic the two-dimensional squares of advertisements on the Internet.

Advertisements are typically known to be annoying to viewers. And so, these worded puzzles that are presented to the user simulate the inconvenience of pop up ads by producing more windows when the user selects the incorrect answer.

As the number of windows accumulate, the more distracted or overwhelmed the user may become, which is intensified by the amount of text on the page. As a result, the reading and comprehension abilities of the viewer will gradually diminish, where they may be distracted and unable to concentrate on the written material. This reflects the shift in our expectations and routine practices in reading that have changed to keep up with the fast-paced mechanisms of the web and its disturbing format.

Net art, which can be described as a form of art that emerged from the revolution of the Internet, was used as the primary source of inspiration for the visual aesthetics. The new medium offered another outlet for creators to address, critique and scrutinise the issues of the web, similarly to the way The Reading Club explores the connection between the body and the digital through the languages of 3D CSS and Javascript.

My name's Flora and I'm currently a student at RMIT University studying design in digital media. I call Melbourne (Australia) my home and aspire to help people who help make the lives of others a little better.

I mainly work with UI/UX, websites and motion graphics, yet I admire many types of creative work, such as architecture and interior design.

All projects featured on my portfolio are original and received a high distinction (the highest possible letter grade) in an academic setting.

Native Plants For Native Bees is a prototype website created on Adobe XD to raise awareness about the declining numbers of native bees in Australia.


Completed — October 2020
Type — Web prototype
Visit — Here

To educate people who don't have much knowledge on gardening but are interested in preserving biodiversity, the prototype showcases a collection of native Australian plants, gardening information and bee resources to encourage the direct actions of growing a bee-friendly garden, or donating to non-profit organisations that support the lives of bees.

Inspired by the form of a visual dictionary, Native Plants For Native Bees presents 18 native Australian flowers that are known to attract native bees and wildlife, and organises the information alphabetically. Using colloquial language, the plants' characteristics are provided in a simplified manner so that'll be easier for novice gardeners to understand.

Users are able to switch between images or list views on the home page as they both have different functions. The main index page features images of the flowers up close, and when the mouse hovers over them, they show what the plant looks like from afar so that users can get a sense of its full size. There's the option to filter the plants into their respective sizes, which helps to categorise the data, and a letter menu that scrolls to the section with plants beginning with that letter.

If users want to compare the plants to each other, they are able view a list and glance over the information to quickly understand the nature of each plant. They are also able to sort the information in ascending or descending order. Plant summaries can also be accessed from either layout when the plant name is clicked on.

The 'how' page explains anything about plants that the user is unsure about. It goes through what the site is about, how to select a plant and what factors to consider, how to use the website to choose a plant, and some gardening tips for the native plants and building a bee-friendly environment.

The 'why' page takes the user through the importance of bees, why they should care about bees, and how they can help including links to organisations they could donate to. A contents section eases accessibility on both pages by breaking down the page into smaller categories.

Green and yellow were the chosen colours to represent plants and bees respectively, and the website. These muted tones harmonise with the strong colours from the images. After initially beginning with Framer Web, Native Plants For Native Bees was finally constructed with Adobe XD instead despite its limitations in functionality.
